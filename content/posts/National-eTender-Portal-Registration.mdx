# How to Register on the National eTender Portal in South Africa

Once you've registered your business on the Central Supplier Database (CSD), the next important step is learning how to find government opportunities.

The National eTender Portal is South Africa's official website for publishing government tender opportunities from national departments, provincial governments, municipalities and many public entities.

Whether you're a construction company, IT provider, consultant, printer, engineer or small business owner, understanding how to use this portal can help you discover opportunities that match your services.

---

# What is the National eTender Portal?

The National eTender Portal is the South African Government's official procurement advertising platform.

It allows businesses to:

- Search available tenders
- Download tender documents
- View briefing session dates
- Check closing dates
- Read bid specifications
- Monitor procurement opportunities

---

# Do You Need to Register?

Most tender opportunities require businesses to have an active CSD registration before they can submit bids.

Although you can browse many opportunities without registration, your business should already be CSD-compliant before responding to tenders.

---

# Before You Begin

Make sure you already have:

- Active CSD Registration
- CIPC Registration
- Tax Compliance Status
- Company Profile
- Contact Details
- Business Email Address

---

# Step 1 – Visit the National eTender Portal

Open the official National eTender Portal in your web browser.

Take a few minutes to familiarise yourself with the homepage and menu options.

---

# Step 2 – Search for Opportunities

Use the search tools to filter tenders by:

- Province
- Government Department
- Industry
- Closing Date
- Keyword

This helps you focus only on opportunities relevant to your business.

---

# Step 3 – Read the Tender Carefully

Never download a tender and immediately begin pricing.

Read:

- Scope of work
- Mandatory requirements
- Required documents
- Evaluation criteria
- Closing instructions

Missing one requirement could disqualify your bid.

---

# Step 4 – Download Supporting Documents

Most tenders include:

- Bid documents
- Pricing schedules
- Technical specifications
- Declaration forms

Download and save everything before starting your submission.

---

# Step 5 – Prepare Your Submission

Before submitting, verify that you have:

- Tax Compliance
- Active CSD
- Company Profile
- B-BBEE documents (where applicable)
- Required certificates
- Signed forms

---

# Common Mistakes

Avoid these common errors:

- Missing the closing date
- Ignoring compulsory briefing sessions
- Forgetting signatures
- Using outdated company information
- Submitting incomplete documentation

---

# Tips for Winning More Opportunities

- Read every page carefully.
- Never rush submissions.
- Organise digital copies of all compliance documents.
- Keep your company profile updated.
- Monitor new tenders regularly.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is the National eTender Portal free?

Yes.

Browsing and downloading tenders is free.

---

## Can startups apply?

Yes. Many opportunities are suitable for qualifying small businesses.

---

## Do I need CSD?

In most cases, yes.

Government buyers generally expect suppliers to have an active CSD registration.
