# What Is Purchase Order Funding? A Complete Guide for South African Businesses

Winning a government tender or receiving a large purchase order should be exciting.

Unfortunately, for many South African businesses, it creates a new problem.

**How do you deliver the goods or services if you don't have enough money to buy stock, pay suppliers, or cover operational costs?**

This is where **Purchase Order Funding (PO Funding)** can help.

Rather than declining the opportunity because of cash flow, many businesses use Purchase Order Funding to fulfil the order and repay the finance once the customer has paid.

---

## What Is Purchase Order Funding?

Purchase Order Funding is a short-term business finance solution that helps companies fulfil confirmed customer orders when they don't have enough working capital.

Instead of giving you an unsecured business loan, the funder assesses the strength of the confirmed purchase order or contract and finances the costs needed to complete the order. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

In many cases, the finance company pays your suppliers directly rather than depositing cash into your business account. Once your customer pays for the completed order, the finance provider is repaid according to the funding agreement. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## A Simple Example

Imagine your company receives a government purchase order worth **R500,000**.

The problem is that you need **R350,000** to buy stock before you can deliver.

You don't have enough cash available.

Instead of declining the contract, you approach a Purchase Order Funding company.

The finance company verifies the purchase order, pays your supplier, and allows you to complete the work.

After delivery, the government department pays your company, and you repay the funder together with the agreed fees.

Without Purchase Order Funding, you may have lost the contract simply because you didn't have enough cash flow.

---

## Why Many Small Businesses Use Purchase Order Funding

Many South African businesses experience a common challenge.

They can win work.

They simply cannot afford to deliver it.

Purchase Order Funding helps bridge that gap by providing working capital specifically for fulfilling confirmed customer orders.

It is commonly used by:

- Wholesalers
- Distributors
- Importers
- Manufacturers
- Government suppliers
- Tender contractors
- Businesses supplying large private companies

## Purchase Order Funding Is Not the Same as a Business Loan

Many people confuse these two.

A traditional business loan is usually based on your company's financial history, profitability, assets, or credit profile.

Purchase Order Funding focuses primarily on the strength of the confirmed order and the ability of the customer to pay once delivery has been completed. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

## Advantages of Purchase Order Funding

Some of the biggest benefits include:

- Accept larger contracts.
- Improve cash flow.
- Avoid declining profitable opportunities.
- Grow your business without large cash reserves.
- Build a stronger trading history.
- Preserve working capital for other business expenses.

---

## Things to Consider

Purchase Order Funding is not free.

Finance companies charge fees for providing the funding.

The exact cost depends on several factors, including:

- Size of the purchase order.
- Risk of the transaction.
- Customer's payment profile.
- Industry.
- Funding period.

Because pricing varies between providers and deals, it's important to compare offers carefully before signing an agreement. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

---

## Does Every Business Qualify?

Not necessarily.

Most funding providers look at factors such as:

- A confirmed purchase order or contract.
- A legitimate customer with the ability to pay.
- An identifiable supplier.
- Company registration documents.
- SARS tax compliance where required.
- Supplier quotations.

Each funding company has its own qualification requirements. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

## From Our Experience

Many small businesses don't lose opportunities because they lack skills.
