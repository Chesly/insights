# How to Qualify for Purchase Order Funding in South Africa

Winning a purchase order is exciting, but receiving payment can take 30, 60 or even 90 days. During that time, you still need money to buy stock, pay suppliers, transport goods and cover operating expenses.

Purchase Order (PO) Funding helps solve this challenge—but not every business automatically qualifies.

Here's what funding providers typically look for before approving your application.

---

## 1. A Genuine Purchase Order

The first requirement is a legitimate purchase order from a reputable customer.

Funding providers will verify:

- The customer issued the PO.
- The order is genuine.
- The customer has a history of paying suppliers.
- The purchase order has not been cancelled.

Government departments, municipalities, SOEs and established private companies are generally viewed as lower-risk customers.

---

## 2. You Must Be a Registered Business

Most lenders expect applicants to have:

- Registered company (Pty Ltd, CC or Sole Proprietor where accepted)
- CIPC registration documents
- Business bank account
- Active business operations

A registered business demonstrates credibility and accountability.

---

## 3. SARS Compliance

One of the biggest approval factors is tax compliance.

Funding providers often request:

- Tax Compliance Status
- Tax PIN
- VAT information (where applicable)

Businesses with unresolved SARS issues may struggle to obtain funding.

---

## 4. A Supplier Quotation

The lender needs to know exactly what will be purchased.

Your quotation should include:

- Supplier details
- Product descriptions
- Quantities
- Prices
- Delivery costs where applicable

This allows the funder to determine how much financing is required.

---

## 5. Profitability

Not every purchase order is worth funding.

The lender calculates whether there is enough profit after considering:

- Cost of goods
- Delivery costs
- Funding fees
- Unexpected expenses

If the profit margin is too small, the application may be declined.

---

## 6. Supplier Reliability

Funding providers also assess your suppliers.

They may verify:

- Supplier legitimacy
- Stock availability
- Delivery capability
- Trading history

Reliable suppliers reduce project risk.

---

## 7. Customer Creditworthiness

The strongest businesses can still be declined if the customer has poor payment practices.

The lender wants confidence that the customer will pay once the goods or services are delivered.

---

## 8. Your Track Record

Previous experience strengthens an application.

Helpful indicators include:

- Completed contracts
- Previous purchase orders
- Existing customers
- Positive references
- Industry experience

New businesses can still qualify, but experienced businesses often receive approvals more easily.

---

## Common Reasons Applications Are Declined

Applications are commonly rejected because of:

- Fake purchase orders
- Incomplete documentation
- Poor SARS compliance
- Unprofitable contracts
- High-risk customers
- Unverified suppliers
- Unrealistic pricing
- Incorrect business information

Preparing everything properly before applying significantly improves your chances.

---

## Tips to Improve Your Approval Chances

Before applying:

- Keep your CIPC documents updated.
- Maintain SARS compliance.
- Build relationships with reliable suppliers.
- Prepare accurate quotations.
- Respond quickly to requests for additional information.
- Keep organised financial records.

A well-prepared business is far more attractive to funding providers.
