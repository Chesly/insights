# How Much Does Purchase Order Funding Cost in South Africa?

One of the first questions business owners ask is:

> **"How much will Purchase Order Funding cost me?"**

The answer depends on several factors, including the size of your purchase order, the customer you're supplying, your supplier, the repayment period and the level of risk involved.

Unlike a traditional business loan, Purchase Order Funding isn't charged like a normal bank loan. Instead, providers charge funding fees based on the transaction itself.

---

## There Is No Standard Fee

Every funding provider has its own pricing model.

The cost usually depends on:

- Purchase order value
- Expected profit margin
- Customer's payment history
- Industry risk
- Funding period
- Supplier location
- Complexity of the transaction

This means two businesses with the same purchase order value could receive completely different funding quotes. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## Typical Fees in South Africa

Many South African providers charge either:

- A once-off transaction fee
- A monthly funding fee
- A percentage of the funded amount
- A percentage of the profit
- Or a combination of these

Some providers advertise pricing from around **10%–20% of the profit** or use deal-specific pricing, while others structure fees based on the transaction's duration and risk rather than a fixed rate. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Always request a written quotation before accepting funding.

---

## What Affects the Price?

Funding generally becomes cheaper when:

- Your customer has an excellent payment history.
- The purchase order comes from a government department or a large corporate.
- Your supplier is verified.
- Your documentation is complete.
- The contract carries a healthy profit margin.

Higher-risk transactions usually attract higher fees.

---

## Don't Only Compare Fees

The cheapest provider isn't always the best.

Also compare:

- Approval speed
- Reputation
- Customer support
- Hidden administration fees
- Early settlement options
- Experience in your industry

A provider that funds within a few days may help you secure a contract you would otherwise lose.

---

## Calculate the Real Cost

Instead of asking:

> "How much does it cost?"

Ask:

> "How much profit will I still make after paying the funding fee?"

If your contract remains profitable after all expenses—including funding—the finance may be well worth using.

---

## Questions to Ask Before Signing

Before accepting any offer, ask:

- What are all the fees?
- Are there hidden charges?
- Who pays my supplier?
- When do I repay the funding?
- What happens if my customer pays late?
- Are there penalties?

Understanding these answers can prevent expensive surprises later.

## Final Thoughts

Purchase Order Funding should be viewed as an investment rather than simply a cost.

If it enables your business to complete a profitable contract, build a relationship with a new customer and grow your turnover, the funding fee may be a worthwhile business expense.

The key is to compare providers carefully, understand every fee and ensure the contract remains profitable before accepting finance.
