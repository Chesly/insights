# 10 Reasons Purchase Order Funding Applications Get Rejected (And How to Avoid Them)

Winning a purchase order is exciting, but receiving funding is never guaranteed.

Funding providers assess every application carefully because they carry financial risk until your customer pays for the completed order.

Understanding why applications are rejected can help you prepare stronger documentation and significantly improve your chances of approval.

---

# 1. The Purchase Order Cannot Be Verified

Funding providers must confirm that the purchase order is genuine.

If they cannot verify it with the customer or issuing organisation, the application will likely be declined.

Always ensure your purchase order is authentic and complete.

---

# 2. Your Customer Has Poor Payment History

Funding companies prefer customers with a proven record of paying suppliers.

If your customer regularly pays late or has financial difficulties, the lender may decline the application.

---

# 3. Your Business Cannot Deliver

Receiving funding does not guarantee you can fulfil the contract.

Providers assess whether your business has the experience, suppliers, staff and operational capacity to complete the work.

---

# 4. Missing Documents

Incomplete applications are one of the biggest causes of delays and rejections.

Common missing documents include:

- Company registration documents
- Tax compliance
- Bank statements
- Supplier quotations
- Signed purchase order

Prepare everything before applying.

---

# 5. Poor Financial Records

Even though purchase order funding focuses on the purchase order itself, lenders still assess your business's financial position.

Accurate bookkeeping increases confidence.

---

# 6. Unrealistic Profit Margins

If the numbers don't make business sense, providers may decline the application.

Ensure your pricing covers:

- Materials
- Labour
- Transport
- Unexpected costs
- Funding fees

---

# 7. Unverified Suppliers

Funding companies often pay suppliers directly.

If your suppliers cannot be verified or appear unreliable, approval may become difficult.

Work with reputable suppliers whenever possible.

---

# 8. Tax or Compliance Issues

Some providers require businesses to be tax compliant before approving finance.

Keeping your SARS obligations up to date strengthens your application.

---

# 9. Applying Too Late

Many businesses only seek funding a few days before delivery.

This leaves little time for verification and due diligence.

Apply as early as possible after receiving the purchase order.

---

# 10. Previous Funding Problems

Businesses with a history of defaulting on funding agreements may struggle to secure new finance.

Maintaining a good relationship with funding providers can make future applications easier.

---

# Tips to Improve Your Chances

Before applying:

- Verify all information.
- Organise your documents.
- Work with reputable suppliers.
- Understand your costs.
- Maintain accurate financial records.
- Apply early.
- Respond quickly to requests for additional information.

Preparation is often the difference between approval and rejection.

---

# Final Thoughts

Purchase order funding is designed to help businesses grow, but providers must manage risk carefully.

By understanding the most common reasons applications are rejected, you can prepare a stronger application and improve your chances of securing the funding needed to fulfil larger contracts.
