# How to Improve Your Chances of Getting Approved for Purchase Order Funding in South Africa

Winning a contract is exciting, but getting the funding to fulfil that contract can be another challenge.

Purchase Order Funding providers want to minimise their risk before approving an application. Understanding what they look for can significantly improve your chances of getting approved.

This guide explains the most important factors lenders consider before funding your purchase order.

---

## What Do Funding Companies Want to See?

Although every funding provider has its own requirements, most evaluate similar areas.

They typically want to know:

- Is the customer legitimate?
- Is the purchase order genuine?
- Can the supplier deliver?
- Is there enough profit in the deal?
- Will the customer pay?

The lower the risk, the higher your chances of approval.

---

## 1. Work With Reputable Customers

Funding providers prefer purchase orders issued by:

- Government departments
- Municipalities
- State-owned entities
- Large corporations
- Established private companies

These organisations generally have a proven payment history, making them less risky.

---

## 2. Keep Your Business Documents Updated

Ensure your business has current documentation, including:

- Company registration documents
- CSD registration (where applicable)
- Tax Compliance Status
- B-BBEE certificate or affidavit
- Bank confirmation letter
- Company profile

Having everything ready speeds up the approval process.

---

## 3. Build a Good Business Track Record

If you've successfully completed previous contracts, keep records such as:

- Purchase orders
- Delivery notes
- Signed completion certificates
- Invoices
- Proof of payment

A strong history shows that your business can deliver.

---

## 4. Price Your Quotes Correctly

Funding providers also examine whether the project will generate sufficient profit.

Avoid:

- Underpricing contracts
- Unrealistic quotations
- Extremely low margins

Healthy profit margins reduce financial risk.

---

## 5. Know Your Suppliers

If your funding depends on buying stock, lenders may verify:

- Supplier quotations
- Product availability
- Delivery times
- Supplier reputation

Reliable suppliers increase confidence in your application.

---

## 6. Maintain Good Financial Records

You don't need to be a large company, but organised financial records help.

Keep:

- Bank statements
- Accounting records
- VAT records (if registered)
- Previous invoices

Professional record-keeping builds trust.

---

## 7. Respond Quickly

Purchase order funding is often time-sensitive.

Prepare documents in advance so you can respond immediately when opportunities arise.

Delays can cause funding approvals to miss customer deadlines.

---

## Common Reasons Applications Are Declined

Funding applications may be rejected if:

- The purchase order cannot be verified.
- Profit margins are too small.
- The customer is considered high risk.
- Business documents are incomplete.
- The supplier cannot deliver.
- The company lacks the capacity to fulfil the contract.

Understanding these risks helps you prepare before applying.

---

## Final Thoughts

Purchase Order Funding is not just about receiving money.

It is about proving that your business can successfully complete the work and that the customer will pay.

Businesses that stay organised, maintain accurate records, and build a professional reputation are far more likely to qualify for funding.
