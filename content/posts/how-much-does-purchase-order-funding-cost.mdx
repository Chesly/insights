# How Much Does Purchase Order Funding Cost in South Africa?

One of the first questions business owners ask is:

> **"How much does purchase order funding cost?"**

The honest answer is that there isn't one fixed price.

Unlike a traditional business loan, purchase order funding is priced according to the specific transaction rather than applying the same interest rate to every business. The cost depends on the size of the deal, the customer, the supplier, the expected payment period and the overall level of risk. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## Why There Isn't a Fixed Price

Every purchase order is different.

A funding company will assess factors such as:

- Value of the purchase order
- Amount of funding required
- Delivery period
- Payment terms
- Industry risk
- Government or private customer
- Your supplier's reliability
- Your company's trading history

A low-risk government contract may receive better pricing than a high-risk private transaction. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## What Fees Can You Expect?

Depending on the funding provider, you may encounter:

- Facility fees
- Administration fees
- Due diligence costs
- Funding fees
- Early settlement fees (where applicable)

Not every provider charges all of these fees, which is why comparing quotes is so important. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## What Is the Typical Cost?

Many South African funding providers indicate that purchase order funding generally falls within a broad range, often around **3% to 8%** of the financed amount, while others structure pricing differently depending on the specific transaction. Some providers quote costs as a percentage of the purchase order, while others calculate fees based on funding duration or risk. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

The important point is this:

**Never assume one company's pricing will be the same as another's.**

Always request a detailed written quotation.

---

## What Makes One Application Cheaper Than Another?

Funding costs are usually lower when:

- The customer has an excellent payment history.
- The purchase order is genuine and easy to verify.
- Delivery is straightforward.
- The payment period is short.
- Your supplier is reliable.
- Your business has successfully completed similar projects before.

The lower the risk, the more competitive the pricing is likely to be. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

---

## Questions You Should Ask Before Accepting Funding

Before signing any agreement, ask:

- What is the total cost?
- Are there administration fees?
- Are there monthly charges?
- What happens if my customer pays late?
- Are there penalties for early repayment?
- Are there any hidden costs?

Never rely on verbal promises.

Request everything in writing.

---

## Should You Compare More Than One Provider?

Absolutely.

Different funding providers assess risk differently.

By comparing multiple quotations, you may save thousands of rand on a single purchase order.

Look beyond the percentage charged.

Also compare:

- Speed of approval
- Reputation
- Customer service
- Experience with government contracts
- Transparency

---

## Final Thoughts

Purchase order funding should never be judged by price alone.

A slightly higher fee may be worthwhile if it enables you to fulfil a large order, satisfy an important customer and generate long-term business opportunities.

The best funding partner is the one that provides transparent pricing, explains every cost clearly and helps your business deliver successfully.
