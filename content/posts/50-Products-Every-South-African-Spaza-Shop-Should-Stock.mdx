# How to Decide What to Stock

Before buying stock, ask yourself:
✅ Do customers ask for it regularly?
✅ Can I sell it quickly?
✅ Does it have a healthy profit margin?
✅ Can I store it safely?
✅ Will customers return to buy it again?

If the answer is **yes** to most of these questions, it's probably worth stocking.

---

# Common Stocking Mistakes

Avoid these common mistakes:
- ❌ Buying too much slow-moving stock.
- ❌ Ignoring seasonal demand.
- ❌ Running out of best-selling products.
- ❌ Stocking products because competitors sell them.
- ❌ Not tracking what customers actually buy.

The best stock decisions are based on customer demand—not guesswork.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## How many products should a new Spaza Shop stock?
Start with your community's most-needed essentials and expand as sales grow. It's better to have 200 products that sell quickly than 1,000 that sit on the shelf.

---

## Which products make the most profit?
Convenience items such as snacks, sweets, cellphone accessories, printing services, and hot beverages often deliver higher profit margins than staple foods.

---

## Should I buy from wholesalers?
Yes. Buying from reputable wholesalers can improve your profit margins and help you keep prices competitive.

---

# Final Thoughts

The best Spaza Shops don't try to sell everything—they focus on selling the **right products** consistently.

Listen to your customers, monitor your sales, and adjust your stock based on what your community actually needs.

A well-stocked shop isn't measured by how full the shelves look, but by how quickly the products move.

---

# Key Takeaways
- Stock fast-moving essentials first.
- High-margin convenience items can significantly increase profits.
- Fresh produce attracts repeat customers.
- Keep your best-selling products available at all times.
- Let customer demand guide your purchasing decisions.
