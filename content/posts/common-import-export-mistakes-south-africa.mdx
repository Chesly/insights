# 5 Common Import & Export Mistakes South African Businesses Make (and How to Avoid Them)

Importing and exporting can unlock exciting growth opportunities for South African businesses, but many first-time traders lose time and money because of avoidable mistakes.

Whether you're importing products to sell locally or exporting South African goods to international markets, understanding the common pitfalls can save you thousands of rands.

---

## 1. Not Registering Before Trading

Many entrepreneurs place overseas orders before obtaining their SARS Import & Export Code.

Without the required registration, your shipment may be delayed while customs verifies your details.

Always register first before placing your first international order. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## 2. Using the Wrong Tariff Code

Every imported and exported product has a Harmonised System (HS) tariff code.

Using the wrong code can result in:

- Incorrect customs duties
- Incorrect VAT calculations
- Delayed clearance
- Penalties
- Customs inspections

Taking the time to identify the correct tariff classification can prevent costly mistakes. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## 3. Importing Restricted Goods Without Permits

Not every product can simply be shipped into South Africa.

Certain goods require permits or approvals before customs will release them.

Examples include:

- Medicines
- Chemicals
- Agricultural products
- Plants
- Animal products
- Certain electronics

Always confirm whether permits are required before placing an order. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

## 4. Incomplete or Incorrect Documentation

Missing invoices, inaccurate product descriptions, or incorrect shipment information are among the leading causes of customs delays.

Before shipping, ensure your paperwork is complete and accurate, including:

- Commercial invoice
- Packing list
- Transport documents
- Product descriptions
- Correct values

Consistent documentation helps goods move through customs more efficiently. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

---

## 5. Underestimating the Total Cost

Many new importers only budget for the purchase price.

However, the total landed cost can also include:

- Shipping
- Insurance
- Customs duties
- Import VAT
- Customs clearance fees
- Storage charges
- Delivery costs

Understanding the full landed cost before buying helps protect your profit margin. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

---

# Final Thoughts

International trade doesn't have to be complicated.

Most costly mistakes happen because businesses rush into importing or exporting without understanding the process.

A little planning before your first shipment can save significant time, money, and stress.
