# Step-by-Step Guide: How to Register for an Import and Export Code in South Africa

If you're planning to import or export goods regularly, obtaining an Import and Export Code from the South African Revenue Service (SARS) should be one of your first priorities.

Fortunately, most applications can be completed online through SARS eFiling using the Registration, Licensing and Accreditation (RLA) system. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

This guide explains the process step by step.

---

## Before You Start

Make sure you have:

- A registered SARS eFiling profile
- Your South African ID or passport
- Your tax reference number
- Proof of address
- Company registration documents (if applicable)
- Banking details

Having these ready will make the application much smoother. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## Step 1: Log in to SARS eFiling

Visit the SARS eFiling portal and log in.

If you don't already have an account, create one first.

---

## Step 2: Link Your Business

If you're applying on behalf of a company:

- Link the business to your individual eFiling profile.
- Ensure you have the correct user rights to submit RLA applications. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## Step 3: Open the Registration, Licensing and Accreditation (RLA) Section

Within eFiling:

- Navigate to the Registration, Licensing and Accreditation (RLA) section.
- Select the appropriate importer or exporter registration option.

The RLA system manages customs registrations electronically. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

---

## Step 4: Complete the Application

You'll be asked to provide:

- Business information
- Contact details
- Banking information
- Nature of your importing or exporting activities

Check everything carefully before submitting.

---

## Step 5: Upload Supporting Documents

SARS may require supporting documentation such as:

- Certified ID copy
- Proof of address
- Company registration documents
- Bank confirmation
- Tax registration information

Missing documents are one of the biggest causes of delays. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

---

## Step 6: Submit Your Application

Review your application carefully.

Once you're satisfied:

- Submit it.
- Keep the confirmation.
- Monitor your SARS eFiling inbox for updates.

---

## Step 7: Wait for SARS Approval

Processing times vary.

If SARS requires additional information, respond as quickly as possible to avoid delays.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Many businesses delay approval because they:

- Upload blurry documents.
- Use outdated proof of address.
- Capture incorrect banking details.
- Forget to link the company to eFiling.
- Submit incomplete applications.

A careful application usually results in a much smoother process.

---

## Final Thoughts

Applying for an Import and Export Code is an important milestone for any South African business planning to trade internationally.

By preparing your documents in advance and following the correct SARS process, you can avoid unnecessary delays and start importing or exporting with confidence.
