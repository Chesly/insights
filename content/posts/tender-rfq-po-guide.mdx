# Government Tender vs RFQ vs Purchase Order: What's the Difference?

Many South African businesses hear terms like **Tender**, **RFQ (Request for Quotation)** and **Purchase Order (PO)** used interchangeably. In reality, they are different stages of the government procurement process.

Understanding how each document fits into the procurement journey can help you prepare better, avoid scams, and improve your chances of winning government work.

Whether you're registering your business for the first time or planning to supply goods and services to government departments, this guide explains how the process works from beginning to end.

---

# The Government Procurement Process

A simplified procurement process typically looks like this:

1. Register your business on the Central Supplier Database (CSD).
2. Register on relevant tender portals.
3. Government advertises an opportunity.
4. Suppliers submit quotations or tender responses.
5. Government evaluates submissions.
6. A successful supplier is selected.
7. A Purchase Order or contract is issued.
8. Goods or services are delivered.
9. Payment is processed according to agreed terms.

Each stage plays an important role.

---

# What Is a Government Tender?

A government tender is a formal invitation for suppliers to submit bids for larger projects or contracts.

Tenders are generally used for:

- Construction projects
- Consulting services
- Long-term supply contracts
- Infrastructure projects
- Municipal services

Tenders usually involve:

- Detailed specifications
- Mandatory documents
- Closing dates
- Evaluation criteria
- Competitive bidding

Winning a tender often results in a formal contract before work begins.

---

# What Is an RFQ?

An RFQ (Request for Quotation) is a request for suppliers to provide prices for specific goods or services.

RFQs are generally:

- Faster than tenders
- Used for lower-value procurement
- Less complex
- Limited to specific requirements

Businesses submit quotations rather than full tender proposals.

Government then evaluates the quotations and selects the best value according to procurement rules.

---

# What Is a Purchase Order?

A Purchase Order (PO) is issued after your quotation or tender has been accepted.

A Purchase Order confirms:

- What government is buying
- Quantities
- Prices
- Delivery location
- Delivery dates
- Payment reference

It gives the supplier official authority to supply the requested goods or services.

---

# Quick Comparison

| Tender | RFQ | Purchase Order |
|---------|-----|----------------|
| Large procurement | Smaller procurement | Final instruction to supply |
| Competitive bids | Quotations | Authorises delivery |
| Formal evaluation | Simplified evaluation | Payment follows delivery |
| Often leads to a contract | May lead directly to a PO | Final procurement document |

# Common Mistakes Suppliers Make

Many businesses assume:

- An RFQ guarantees work.
- A Purchase Order is the same as a tender.
- Every Purchase Order is genuine.
- They can supply government without registering first.

These misunderstandings often result in missed opportunities—or worse, procurement fraud.

---

# Tips for New Suppliers

If you're new to government procurement:

- Register on the Central Supplier Database (CSD).
- Register on the National eTender Portal.
- Monitor procurement opportunities regularly.
- Keep your company documents updated.
- Verify every Purchase Order before supplying goods.
- Never pay "registration" or "activation" fees requested by scammers.

Preparation gives your business a much better chance of success.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Can I receive a Purchase Order without winning a tender?

Yes. Smaller procurements often begin with an RFQ rather than a formal tender.

## Is every RFQ published publicly?

No. Some RFQs are sent directly to selected suppliers, depending on procurement rules and thresholds.

## Does a Purchase Order guarantee payment?

A legitimate Purchase Order forms part of the procurement process, but payment is still subject to delivery, acceptance, and government payment procedures.
