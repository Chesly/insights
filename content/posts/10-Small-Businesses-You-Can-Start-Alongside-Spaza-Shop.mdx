A successful Spaza Shop doesn't have to rely only on selling bread, milk and groceries. Many of the busiest township stores make extra income by offering services that people use every day.

The best part? Most of these businesses require very little extra space and can use the customers already walking into your shop.

If you're looking for ways to increase your monthly income, these ideas are a great place to start.

> 💡 **Quick Tip**
> You don't need to start all ten businesses. Start with one that fits your community, master it, then expand as demand grows.

---

# Why Add Another Business?

Adding another income stream helps you:
- 💰 Increase your daily profits.
- 👥 Attract new customers.
- 🛒 Encourage shoppers to buy more while visiting.
- 📈 Grow your business without opening another shop.
- ❤️ Become more valuable to your community.

Small changes can make a big difference over time.

---

# 1. Sell Airtime, Data and Electricity

Almost everyone buys airtime or prepaid electricity regularly.

Offering these services means customers have another reason to visit your shop.

You can sell:

- Airtime
- Data bundles
- Prepaid electricity
- Water vouchers
- Gaming vouchers

These services require very little space and can generate income every day.

---

# 2. Become a Courier Collection Point

Online shopping continues to grow in South Africa.

Many customers prefer collecting parcels close to home instead of travelling long distances.

Your shop could become a convenient collection point for courier companies.

Benefits include:

- More daily foot traffic
- New customers
- Extra income opportunities
- Increased grocery sales while customers collect parcels

---

# 3. Install Parcel Lockers

As online shopping grows, secure parcel lockers are becoming more popular.

Customers can collect packages at any time without waiting for deliveries at home.

This service can attract people who may become regular customers.

---

# 4. Offer Printing and Photocopying
Many learners, students and job seekers need affordable printing services.

You could offer:

- Black and white printing
- Colour printing
- Photocopying
- Scanning
- CV printing
- Document laminating

This business is especially valuable near schools, colleges and government offices.

---

# 5. Open a Coffee or Tea Station

People love grabbing a quick hot drink on their way to work or school.

Even a small coffee station can generate additional income throughout the day.

You can also sell:

- Vetkoek
- Muffins
- Doughnuts
- Scones
- Kota breakfasts

These products often encourage impulse purchases.

---

# 6. Sell Wi-Fi Vouchers or Create a Wi-Fi Hotspot

Internet access is essential for work, education and entertainment.

You can provide:
- Wi-Fi vouchers
- Daily internet access
- Hourly internet packages

This service can attract students, remote workers and local residents.

> 📶 **Did You Know?**
> Affordable community Wi-Fi has become one of the fastest-growing services in many South African townships.

---

# 7. Sell Cellphone Accessories

Almost everyone owns a smartphone.

Popular products include:
- Chargers
- USB cables
- Earphones
- Phone covers
- Screen protectors
- Power banks

These items are relatively inexpensive to stock and often have healthy profit margins.

---

# 8. Stock School Uniforms and Stationery
Parents often prefer buying school essentials close to home.

Consider stocking:
- School shirts
- Socks
- Jerseys
- Exercise books
- Pens
- Pencils
- School bags

Sales usually increase before each school term begins.

---

# 9. Offer Local Delivery
Many customers are willing to pay for convenience.

A simple delivery service can help:
- Elderly customers
- Busy parents
- Local businesses
- Customers ordering large grocery items

Even deliveries within a few kilometres can create another source of income.

---

# 10. Sell Fresh Produce

Fresh fruit and vegetables are always in demand.

Popular products include:

- Tomatoes
- Potatoes
- Onions
- Bananas
- Apples
- Spinach
- Cabbage

Buying directly from local farmers or fresh produce markets may help improve your profit margins.

Fresh produce also encourages customers to visit your shop more often.

---

# Which Business Should You Start First?

Ask yourself these questions:
✅ What do people in my community ask for most?
✅ Which service is missing nearby?
✅ What can I afford to start?
✅ Which business fits inside my available space?

Start small, learn what works and expand over time.

---

> 🚀 **Remember**
> Some of South Africa's biggest businesses started by solving one small problem in their community.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Do I need a lot of money to start another business?
Not always. Some ideas, like selling airtime or offering local deliveries, require very little upfront investment.

---

## Which business makes the most profit?
That depends on your location and customer demand. Services like printing, cellphone accessories and coffee often offer good profit margins.

---

## Can I run more than one side business?
Absolutely. Many successful Spaza Shops combine several of these ideas to create multiple income streams.

---

# Final Thoughts

Your Spaza Shop already brings customers through the door. The question is: **how many other needs can you meet while they're there?**

Adding just one extra service could increase your daily income, attract new customers and help your business stand out from nearby competitors.

Start with one idea, deliver excellent service, and keep improving as your business grows.

---

# Key Takeaways
- A Spaza Shop can generate income from more than just groceries.
- Start with one additional service before expanding.
- Choose businesses that solve problems in your community.
- Low-cost ideas like airtime, printing and deliveries can produce consistent income.
- The businesses that adapt to customer needs are often the ones that grow the fastest.
