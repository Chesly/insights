# Purchase Order Funding vs Business Loans: Which Is Better for South African Businesses?

Many business owners assume they need a business loan whenever they need money. In reality, that isn't always the smartest option.

If your business has already secured a purchase order from a government department, municipality or large private company, **purchase order funding** may be a faster and more affordable solution.

Understanding the difference could save your business thousands of rand.

---

## What Is a Business Loan?

A business loan is money borrowed from a bank or finance company that must be repaid over an agreed period with interest.

The lender usually looks at:

- Credit history
- Financial statements
- Cash flow
- Existing debt
- Business performance
- Assets that can be used as security

Approval may take several days or even weeks.

---

## What Is Purchase Order Funding?

Purchase order funding is not a traditional loan.

Instead of lending money based mainly on your financial history, the finance company looks at the purchase order you've received from a reputable customer.

If approved, they usually pay your supplier directly so you can deliver the goods.

Once your customer pays the invoice, the funding company deducts its agreed fees and transfers the balance to your business.

---

## Quick Comparison

| Purchase Order Funding | Business Loan |
|-------------------------|---------------|
| Based mainly on an approved purchase order | Based on your financial position |
| Usually faster approval | Approval may take longer |
| Designed for specific orders | Can be used for many business purposes |
| Supplier often paid directly | Money paid to your business |
| Short-term funding | Short, medium or long-term funding |
| Repaid when customer pays | Monthly repayments |

---

## When Purchase Order Funding Makes More Sense

Purchase order funding is ideal when:

- You already have a confirmed purchase order.
- You don't have enough cash to buy stock.
- Your customer has a good payment history.
- The opportunity is larger than your available cash flow.
- You need funding quickly.

---

## When a Business Loan Makes More Sense

A business loan may be the better choice if you need money for:

- Buying equipment
- Expanding your premises
- Hiring employees
- Marketing
- Purchasing vehicles
- Long-term business growth

---

## Which Costs More?

Neither option is automatically cheaper.

A bank loan generally charges interest over time.

Purchase order funding companies usually charge a funding fee based on the value of the transaction and the level of risk involved.

Before signing any agreement, ask for:

- Total funding cost
- Administration fees
- Early settlement rules
- Hidden charges
- Repayment terms

Always compare more than one funding provider.

---

## Can You Qualify for Both?

Yes.

Many established businesses use both.

For example:

- A business loan helps buy machinery.
- Purchase order funding helps fulfil a large government order.

Each solves a different problem.

---

## Common Mistakes

Avoid these mistakes:

- Applying for a loan when purchase order funding is more suitable.
- Accepting the first funding offer without comparing providers.
- Not understanding all fees.
- Signing agreements without reading the terms.
- Taking funding for orders you cannot realistically deliver.

---

## Final Thoughts

The best funding option depends on your situation.

If you already have a confirmed purchase order but lack the working capital to fulfil it, purchase order funding can help your business grow without waiting months to save enough cash.

If you're investing in the long-term growth of your company, a traditional business loan may be more appropriate.

The key is choosing the right funding tool for the right opportunity.
