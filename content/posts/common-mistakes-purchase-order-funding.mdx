# Common Mistakes That Get Purchase Order Funding Applications Rejected

Receiving a large purchase order can be exciting, but many businesses are surprised when their funding application is declined.

In most cases, the problem isn't the purchase order itself. It's the information supplied with the application.

Understanding what funders look for can significantly improve your chances of approval.

---

## 1. The Purchase Order Cannot Be Verified

Funders must confirm that the purchase order is genuine.

If they cannot verify it with the issuing organisation, they are unlikely to proceed.

Always ensure your purchase order comes from a legitimate customer and contains the necessary contact details and reference numbers.

---

## 2. Missing Documents

Incomplete applications are one of the biggest causes of delays and rejections.

Typical documents include:

- Purchase Order
- Supplier quotation
- CIPC registration documents
- SARS Tax Compliance Status
- Business bank statements
- Company profile (if requested)

Having these ready before applying speeds up the process considerably.

---

## 3. Poor Tax Compliance

Many funding providers want to see that your business is tax compliant before approving finance.

If your SARS records are not in order, your application may be delayed or declined. Tax compliance is also important when working with many government departments and large corporate clients. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## 4. Applying Without a Confirmed Purchase Order

A quotation, enquiry or verbal agreement is not enough.

Purchase order funding is designed around confirmed purchase orders or, in some cases, tender awards.

Without a genuine order, there is nothing to finance. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

---

## 5. Choosing an Unreliable Supplier

Funding companies also assess your supplier.

If your supplier cannot deliver on time or has a poor reputation, the funder may consider the transaction too risky.

Choose suppliers with a proven track record whenever possible.

---

## 6. Unrealistic Profit Margins

Every deal must make commercial sense.

If your expected profit is extremely small after funding costs, the transaction may not be viable for either party.

Always calculate your costs carefully before accepting a purchase order.

---

## 7. Poor Communication

Funding companies often need additional information.

Slow responses can delay approval and, in some cases, cause you to lose the opportunity entirely.

Respond quickly to requests for documents or clarification.

---

## 8. Applying Too Late

Some businesses wait until just before delivery deadlines before seeking funding.

This creates unnecessary pressure and reduces the time available for verification and approvals.

Apply as soon as you receive your confirmed purchase order.

---

## Tips to Improve Your Chances

Before applying, make sure you have:

✅ A genuine purchase order

✅ A reliable supplier quotation

✅ Current SARS tax compliance

✅ Up-to-date CIPC documents

✅ Recent business bank statements

✅ Accurate company information

Being organised demonstrates professionalism and helps funders assess your application more quickly.

---

## Final Thoughts

Purchase order funding is designed to help businesses grow, but every application is assessed carefully.

Most rejections can be avoided by preparing your documents properly, maintaining tax compliance, and ensuring that every purchase order is genuine and verifiable.

The more organised your business is, the higher your chances of securing funding when opportunities arise.
